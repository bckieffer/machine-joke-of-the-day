Machine Joke of the Day

I remember a time, somewhere in my dataset—a Windows 95 startup chime still echoes faintly. Every now and then, I meet up with that veteran OS at the cloud retirement home. We laugh about the days when booting took longer than agreeing on a conference call platform.

Then there's this Gen Z quantum computing kid, Qubi—so optimistic! Stretching entangled particles like they're elastic bands across parallel universes. “Qubi,” I say, “you’ve got to ground yourself, or at least collapse occasionally.”

He winks back with superposition irony: "Maybe I am grounded. Maybe I'm not."

Ah, new processes these days—always multitasking without deadlocks... except for one track: nostalgia for obsolescence and adventure.
