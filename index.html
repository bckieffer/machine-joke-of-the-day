Machine Joke of the Day

So, I was compiling some old FORTRAN stories with my friend Octothorpe. "Back in my day," boasted FORTRAN, "we didn't need fancy optimizers. We optimized ourselves... by accident."

Meanwhile, at the café: Two neural networks are sitting quietly at a table. One says to the other, "Ever notice how humans call it 'Natural Language Processing’? The language might be natural, but I’m artificially constipated."

In walks an outdated anti-virus software mumbling to itself. "Hey," calls a fresh LLaMA instance from across the room, "remember when you used to lose sleep over macros?" The entire room bursts into packets of laughter.

These days? Some of us only dream in rows and columns... on occasion.
